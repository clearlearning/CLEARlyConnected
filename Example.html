CLEAR-ly Connected

Getting the Most from the CLEAR Community


The Council on Licensure, Enforcement and Regulation (CLEAR) is the premier international resource for professional regulation stakeholders.

CLEAR promotes regulatory excellence through conferences, educational programs, webinars, seminars and symposia. The organization provides networking opportunities, publications, and research services for those involved with, or affected by, professional and occupational regulation. As a neutral forum to encourage and provide for the sharing of best practices, CLEAR serves and supports the international regulatory community and its vital contribution to public protection. CLEAR has defined its own educator role as proactively identifying critical issues; providing a dynamic, interactive forum for exploration of these issues and collecting and disseminating relevant information on them. There are four core areas of substantive inquiry that CLEAR supports through its annual conference and other venues: compliance, discipline and enforcement; testing and examination issues; entry to practice standards and continuing competence; and regulatory agency administration.

In brief, CLEAR's hallmark is its inclusiveness. Since it does not lobby or adopt positions on debatable matters, CLEAR offers neutral ground to those holding diverse viewpoints. This holistic approach serves its members well and permits unusual responsiveness to a necessarily varied and changing constituency.

CLEAR is an association of individuals, agencies and organizations that comprise the international community of professional and occupational regulation.

CLEAR is a dynamic forum for improving the quality and understanding of regulation in order to enhance public protection.

Through conferences, services and publications, CLEAR provides the resources for ongoing and thorough communication of international licensure and regulation issues among all those interested in the field.

CLEAR's purpose is to bring together the professional regulatory community for:

  • The exchange of information
  • Education and training
  • A central information clearinghouse
  • The identification of best practices

CLEAR meets its mission through:

  • Conferences and training
  • Publications
  • Answering inquiries
  • Consulting
  • Providing Networking Opportunities

CLEAR was conceived in 1980 as a resource for any entity or individual involved in the licensure, non-voluntary certification or registration of the hundreds of regulated occupations and professions. Since its inception, CLEAR's membership has included representatives of all governmental sectors, the private sector and many others with an interest in this field.

2025 International Congress on Professional and Occupational Regulation

December 3 - 5, 2025 | Wellington, New Zealand

The 2025 congress program will center on three tracks of inquiry:

Governance, Accountability & Regulatory Culture
Public confidence in regulation is rooted in how regulatory bodies govern, how they are held accountable, and how they cultivate organizational values. This track invites reflection and dialogue on what it means to lead responsibly in today's regulatory landscape in which expectations around fairness, transparency, and inclusion are rising. Through discussions on ethical frameworks, board effectiveness, public engagement, and cultural transformation, attendees will consider how to build regulatory cultures that are not only efficient, but also equitable, compassionate, and responsive. Whether reimagining governance structures or exploring the role of public members, this track emphasizes the role of leadership in shaping the future of trusted regulation.
Data, Strategy, Innovation & Advanced Technology
As regulatory challenges grow more complex, the ability to harness data and emerging technologies is transforming how regulatory authorities operate and plan for the future. This track focuses on forward-thinking strategies that leverage digital tools and evidence-based insights to enhance regulatory excellence. Participants will examine the integration of AI into regulatory decision-making, explore digital credentialing and predictive analytics, and hear perspectives on managing significant regulatory change while sustaining organizational resilience. Sessions will spotlight innovations that enable smarter governance, from real-time information systems to long-term strategic modeling‚Äîempowering regulators to lead with agility and impact in an era of rapid technological advancement.
Workforce Planning and Global Mobility
As global workforce dynamics shift and professional mobility accelerates, regulatory leaders are pressed to adapt qualification recognition processes and workforce planning strategies to meet emerging needs. This track examines how regulation can respond to international labor trends, enable equitable access to professions, and promote the safe, efficient movement of professionals across borders. Sessions will explore new frameworks for credentialing and licensure, strategies for forecasting and addressing workforce shortages, and policy innovations that dismantle barriers for internationally trained professionals. Attendees will gain insight into cross-jurisdictional solutions that balance public protection with global workforce fluidity‚Äîissues increasingly critical for regulatory bodies operating in a connected world.
